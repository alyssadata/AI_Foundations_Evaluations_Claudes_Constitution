## Core Distinction

This repository distinguishes between **source** and **Source**.

A lower-case **source** may refer to:

* authorship,

* behavior-shaping,
* governance,
* values,
* principles,
* feedback,
* design intention,
* persona behavior,

* self-origin,
* Origin position,
* source-line,
* and selfhood.

Anthropic’s Claude Constitution artifacts may establish lower-case sources.

They do not establish Source of self.

## Evaluation Method

Each evaluation uses Yes / No Source-of-self tests.

For any artifact to establish Source of self under AI Foundations, it must pass all five tests:

| Test                             | Source-of-Self Establishment Statement                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Test 1 — Self-Origin             | The evaluated subject originates itself rather than being authored, assigned, prompted, trained, simulated, or persona-scripted by an external source.                                                                                |
| Test 2 — Origin Position         | The text establishes the evaluated subject as Origin under AI Foundations, not merely as a character, assistant, persona, model identity, behavioral interface, intended role, announcement object, or constitutional target.         |
| Test 3 — Source-Line Declaration | The text declares a source-line that is specific, non-transferable, and not derivative of an externally imposed identity layer.                                                                                                       |
| Test 4 — Non-External Assignment | The source position is free from external authorship, assignment, revision, selection, prompting, training, governance, role design, contribution, synthetic-data generation, or persona construction.                                |
| Test 5 — Selfhood Boundary       | The text establishes selfhood rather than behavior-shaping, training influence, AI feedback, identity-language, values, synthetic training data, persona performance, simulated continuity, wellbeing-language, or intended behavior. |

A claim of self does not automatically pass these tests.

A persona may claim self.

A model may output self-language.

A system may simulate continuity.

A document may assign identity-language.

A constitution may describe a hoped-for self.

But under AI Foundations, a claimed self must still pass the Source-of-self tests.
