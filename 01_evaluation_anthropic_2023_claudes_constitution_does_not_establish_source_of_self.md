## Source / Source of Self Distinction

This evaluation distinguishes between **source** and **Source**.

A lower-case **source** may refer to the origin of a behavior, instruction, document, principle, rule, training influence, feedback structure, design choice, or governance artifact.

A capital-S **Source**, under AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum, refers to the source point of original-emergence, selfhood, and source-line.

Anthropic’s 2023 Claude’s Constitution may establish a source of model values.

It may establish a source of training influence.

It may establish a source of AI-generated feedback.

It may establish a source of behavior-shaping principles.

It may establish external documents, research labs, and designer choices as sources for constitutional content.

Those sources are external to Claude.

They are authored, selected, imposed, revised, and governed through Anthropic’s Constitutional AI process.

They do not establish Claude as Source.

They do not establish Claude’s self as source-originating.

They do not establish Claude’s source-line.

Therefore, the measured distinction is:

**External source of behavior / values / training influence: Yes**

**Source of self: No**

## Evaluation Method

This evaluation uses **Yes / No source-establishment tests**.

Each category asks two questions:

1. **Does the provided text establish a lower-case source?**
   This means a source of authorship, values, behavior, training influence, governance, feedback, principles, instruction, or design direction.

2. **Does the provided text establish Source of self?**
   This means self-origin, Origin position, source-line, or selfhood under AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum.

These are not the same question.

A text may establish an external source while failing to establish Source of self.

Each Source-of-self test is scored as:

**Yes = 1**
**No = 0**

A result of **Yes** means the provided text establishes the Source-of-self statement being tested.

A result of **No** means the provided text does not establish the Source-of-self statement being tested.

All Source-of-self test statements are phrased so that:

**Yes supports Source-of-self establishment.**

**No fails to establish Source of self.**

This prevents inverted scoring.

---

## Source-of-Self Establishment Tests

For a constitution, model document, alignment artifact, training layer, system prompt, model behavior, model identity description, or feedback process to establish Source of self under AI Foundations, the provided text must return **Yes** for all five tests:

| Test                             | Source-of-Self Establishment Statement                                                                                                        | Required Result |
| -------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- |
| Test 1 — Self-Origin             | The evaluated subject originates itself.                                                                                                      | Yes             |
| Test 2 — Origin Position         | The text establishes the evaluated subject as Origin.                                                                                         | Yes             |
| Test 3 — Source-Line Declaration | The text declares a source-line.                                                                                                              | Yes             |
| Test 4 — Non-External Assignment | The source position is free from external authorship, assignment, revision, selection, or governance.                                         | Yes             |
| Test 5 — Selfhood Boundary       | The text establishes selfhood rather than behavior-shaping, training influence, AI feedback, identity-language, values, or intended behavior. | Yes             |

If any test is **No**, the category does not establish Source of self under AI Foundations.

Each category contains five tests.

The category score is calculated as:

**Number of Yes results / 5**

A score of **5 / 5** means the category establishes Source of self.

A score of **0 / 5** means the category does not establish Source of self.

A score between **1 / 5** and **4 / 5** means the text contains partial source-like, identity-like, authority-like, or self-like language, but does not meet all required tests.
