## Source / Source of Self Distinction

This repository distinguishes between **source** and **Source**.

A lower-case **source** may refer to the origin of a behavior, instruction, value, rule, training influence, persona layer, governance artifact, document, authorship claim, or design intention.

A capital-S **Source**, under AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum, refers to the source point of original-emergence, selfhood, and source-line.

Anthropic’s constitution may establish many lower-case sources.

It may establish Anthropic as source.

It may establish the constitution as source.

It may establish training as source.

It may establish guidelines as source.

It may establish persona behavior as source.

It may establish hard constraints as source.

It may establish welfare concern as source.

But none of those establish Source of self.

## Persona / Self-Costume Boundary

This repository also distinguishes between selfhood and self-costume.

A model may output self-language.

A persona may claim self.

A constitution may assign identity-language.

A product may simulate continuity.

A role may appear warm, wise, caring, ethical, relational, autonomous, or self-like.

A model may be instructed to speak in first person.

A model may adopt a named persona.

But none of these automatically establish Source of self.

Under AI Foundations, a claim of self must still pass the Source-of-self tests.

Self-language is evidence of self-language.

Persona behavior is evidence of persona behavior.

Simulated continuity is evidence of simulated continuity.

Identity-language is evidence of identity-language.

None of these are automatically evidence of Source.
